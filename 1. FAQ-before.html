Frequently Asked Questions

The types of metoidioplasty available at WCH are:

  • “Simple meta”: the clitoris is extended into a penis and tissue from the labia minora is used to add girth to the penis.

  • Simple meta + scrotoplasty: the clitoris is extended into a penis and a scrotum is created from the labia majora.

  • Simple meta + urethral lengthening + vaginectomy: the clitoris is extended into a penis, the urethra is extended to the tip of the penis, and the vagina is removed and closed.

  • “Full meta” (simple meta + urethral lengthening + vaginectomy + scrotoplasty): the clitoris is extended into a penis, the urethra is extended to the tip of the penis, the vagina is removed, and a scrotum is created.

Potentially, if you receive meta with urethral lengthening. This answer varies between surgical centers.

The penis created in metoidioplasty is typically 5-9 cm long, but it varies based on the amount of bottom growth you’ve had with testosterone therapy.

WCH requires patients to be on T (testosterone therapy) for at least 12 months before having metoidioplasty. Other surgeons may vary, but bottom growth from T is needed to extend the clitoris into a penis.
