After Surgery

Frequently Asked Questions

At WCH, patients getting simple meta spend one night in the hospital, and they are discharged the morning after surgery. Patients getting full meta spend 2-3 nights in the hospital.

Most people return to work in 6-8 weeks after simple meta and 8-12 weeks after full meta. However, this varies based on your individual healing process. It’s recommended to plan for at least 8 weeks off work.

These estimates may vary based on your recovery process, but generally speaking, you can resume all normal day-to-day activities 8 weeks after surgery. You can return to all physical activities, including lifting, exercising, and sexual activity, 12 weeks after surgery.

Like any surgery, there is significant risk for complications after meta. However, the complication rate varies by the surgery you have, your surgeon’s techniques, and your own healing process. In general, the risk of complications is higher if you get meta with urethral lengthening. Some possible complications are serious, requiring a follow-up surgery, but the most common complications are minor and usually resolve on their own.

If you don’t get urethral lengthening, you will not be able to stand to pee after meta. If you get urethral lengthening, you may be able to stand to pee after meta, but individual experiences vary.
