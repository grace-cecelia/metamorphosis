Consultations help your surgical team understand your current health and your surgery goals. These appointments are a chance to ask questions, express doubts and concerns, and figure out which specific surgery is right for you.

Note: information on this page is specific to the consult process at Women’s College Hospital. However, much of the information may still be informative for people outside of WCH.

Once your funding is approved for your surgery, a referral will be sent to your chosen surgical center. If the referral is accepted, the surgical team will contact you to schedule an initial consultation.

You will have a virtual consultation with the nurse followed by an in-person visit with the surgeon. Additional visits with other TRS team members may be required and additional information might be sought from your healthcare providers or other specialists. WCH patients will receive educational materials through MyChart that should be reviewed.

Nurse Consultation

Once the TRS team accepts your referral, you’ll schedule a virtual consultation with a nurse practitioner. For this, you will need access to a device that supports Zoom and an internet connection.

In this appointment, they will take you through a presentation explaining the following:

  • Who the TRS team is
  • Some of the appointments you’ll attend
  • Your options for surgery and how they work, including photos and videos
  • Preparing for surgery
  • Recovery
  • Complications
  • Your questions and concerns

During your consult, the nurse will also have some questions for you about your health history. These questions help them understand how you might need to prepare for surgery. To answer their questions, information you might want to have on hand includes:

  • Medications you take, including doses
  • Any allergies
  • Previous surgeries you’ve had, including date and location or surgeon
  • Medical conditions and names of any specialists you see for treatment
  • Your preferred pharmacy’s information (name, location, phone number, fax number)
  • Your mental health care providers
  • Your specific goals, questions, and concerns about surgery

Surgical Consultation

The surgical consultation is an in-person visit to WCH to meet the surgeon. This appointment is your opportunity to discuss your specific goals with the surgeon. The surgeon will ask you:

  • Your options for surgery and how they work
  • What you want your genitals to look like
  • How you want your genitals to work
  • If you have any concerns about the surgery or current health issues
  • Your specific questions and concerns

The consultation is a good time to bring up the questions you have thought of since your virtual consultation with the nurse.

Your surgery can be booked once all the health information needed has been collected, there are no unaddressed concerns, and a surgery date becomes available.

Physical Exam

An important part of the consultation is the physical exam. The steps of the physical exam are as follows:

  1. The surgeon leaves the room to give you privacy.
  2. You remove your clothes from the waist down.
  3. You lie down on the exam table with a sheet over you.
  4. The surgeon knocks, re-enters, and puts your feet up into the stirrups to begin the examination.
  5. The surgeon measures your bottom growth and examines your urethra and vagina (if you still have one). No speculum is used.
  6. The surgeon will talk you through this part. You are in full control and can tell them to stop at any time. Their goal is to make sure you’re as comfortable through this process as possible.
  7. This part only takes about 1-2 minutes.
  8. The surgeon may take a picture of your genitals to help them plan your surgery.
  9. The surgeon leaves the room so you can put your clothes back on.

Genital exams can be a major source of dysphoria and/or anxiety. The surgeons are aware of this and will work with you to ensure you feel as safe as possible through the process. Here are a few strategies for regulating anxiety during the process:

  • Deep breathing exercises
  • Listen to music or a podcast to distract yourself
  • Bring a fidget toy or stress ball to keep your hands busy
  • A hand mirror is available if you would like to see what the surgeon is doing

Pre-Surgical Anatomy

During the physical exam, the surgeon will look at your genitals to see your current anatomy and assess whether you are eligible for surgery. Remember, your pre-surgical anatomy is the strongest predictor of your post-surgical anatomy. You may hear your surgeon refer to factors such as:

  • Limited bottom growth: your clitoris has not grown much, even after taking T.
  • Small labia minora: the inner folds of skin in your genitals are small.
  • Larger mons or higher belly fat: the fat above your genitals or fat in your belly partially covers your genitals.

These factors don’t automatically mean you’re ineligible for surgery. However, they may impact your satisfaction with the result of your surgery. Just remember that everyone has a different experience, and even if your genitals look like the pictures here, your results might be different.

Most people are happy with the results of their meta, even if they don’t have the “ideal” pre-surgical anatomy. The TRS team is there to work with you, not against you. If meta is your goal, they will do their best to help you achieve it.
