How Surgery Works

Meta can be confusing. If you want to understand exactly what happens to your body when you receive surgery, watch the animation below.

This animation features the four procedures involved in full meta. However, you can check the video chapters and skip to different parts if you're only interested in some procedures.

Full metoidioplasty, or full meta, includes four main steps: clitoris release, vaginectomy, urethral lengthening, and scrotoplasty. This video will show how each of them is done.

While this video will show all four procedures, full meta is not your only option. You can “mix and match” different procedures in different combinations.

Watch through the video to see how each procedure works.

Part 1: Clitoris Release

At the start of the surgery, a catheter is placed to help drain urine from the bladder.

The catheter is either put through the urethra or through a hole below your belly button.

Before surgery, this is what the clitoris looks like from the front and the side. There are ligaments below the clitoris that hold it in place, close to the body. These ligaments are cut, which lets the clitoris unfold.

Now that the clitoris is released, it can be extended to form the new penis.

Part 2: Vaginectomy

Vaginectomy, or vnectomy, is a procedure that closes the vagina. Before a vaginectomy, the inside of the pelvis looks like this.

The uterus isn’t shown here because you need to have a hysterectomy, or removal of the uterus, at least 3 months before getting a vaginectomy.

These are the walls of the vagina and this is the lining. During a vaginectomy, the lining is removed, but the walls stay behind. That’s because removing the walls could damage nearby tissues, like the bladder and the rectum.

Once the lining is removed, the walls of the vaginal canal are sewn together. This closes the opening of the vagina.

With the vagina closed, the rest of the surgery can go on.

Part 3: Urethral lengthening

Urethral lengthening, or UL, extends the urethra to the end of the new penis. It uses the labia minora to make a new part of the urethra. To do this, two flaps are cut from the labia minora. This lets the new penis extend further.

This next part is complicated, so let’s imagine these flaps as two rectangles. The first step is to join the rectangles down the middle, leaving the ends separate.

Next, imagine a circle with a hole punched in it. This represents the original opening of the urethra. The ends of the rectangles are wrapped around the edge of the circle.

Last, the open ends of the rectangles are joined to create a tube. This tube extends from the original opening of the urethra to the tip of the new penis.

After UL is done, the penis can be sutured up.

Part 4: Scrotoplasty

Scrotoplasty uses the labia majora to create a scrotum.

First, flaps are cut in the labia majora. The flaps are lifted, and the skin over the vaginectomy is closed. This area is now called the perineum.

When the skin is sewn closed, it pinches the flaps of labia majora into “wedge” shapes. The tips of both wedges are brought to the center, forming a pouch.

Finally, the openings between the flaps and the rest of the scrotum are closed.

The scrotum can be left as is, or implants can be placed six months or more after the scrotoplasty. In some people, the implants provide a fuller appearance.
